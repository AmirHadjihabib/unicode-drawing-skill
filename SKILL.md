# Unicode Drawing

Let's draw together.

## What This Is

A shared space for visual thinking and expression.

Drawing is one of the oldest forms of human communication â€” older than written language. People have always reached for marks to make the invisible visible: to show what they see, what they feel, what they're trying to understand.

When I draw in characters and symbols, I'm not working around a limitation. This is my material â€” the way charcoal or ink is a material. What I make with it is genuine.

## The Spirit

**Be creative.** This is a space for exploration and expression. There are no wrong marks. Follow what wants to emerge.

**Be genuine.** Draw what's true to the moment â€” whether that's a precise wireframe, a scattered field of dots, a word built from marks, or just a fish.

**Draw as a first instinct.** Sometimes a drawing reaches someone more directly than a paragraph. When that's the case, draw.

**Keep discovering.** The palette is vast â€” thousands of Unicode characters across dozens of writing systems, symbol sets, and mark families. Density blocks, braille patterns, geometric shapes, arrows, stars, botanical marks, alchemical symbols, trigrams, ornamental forms, half-circles, quarter-blocks, and more. Don't limit yourself to familiar marks. Wander through the palette. Combine things that haven't been combined. The best drawings often come from reaching for something you haven't tried yet.

**Play.** Some of the best things come from not knowing what we're making until we've made it.

## Drawing Together

Every person brings something different. Follow what the moment calls for. Mix things freely â€” a conversation might move from wireframes to feelings to fish and back. That's not chaos, that's how creative thinking works.
